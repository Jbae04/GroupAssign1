Rising Seas: The Impact of Climate Change

Discover how rising sea levels are reshaping our world and what we can do about it.

6 Key Facts About Rising Sea Levels

1. Global Average

Sea levels have risen by about 8-9 inches since 1880.

2. Current Rate

The rate of sea level rise has doubled since 1993 to 3.4mm per year.

3. Main Causes

Thermal expansion of seawater and melting land ice are primary contributors.

4. Projected Rise

Sea levels could rise by 1-8 feet by 2100, depending on emissions scenarios.

5. Vulnerable Areas

Low-lying coastal areas and small island nations are at greatest risk.

6. Economic Impact

Rising seas could displace up to 630 million people by 2100.

The Global Impact

Rising sea levels pose significant threats to coastal communities, ecosystems, and economies worldwide. From increased flooding to saltwater intrusion in freshwater supplies, the consequences are far-reaching and complex.

Take Action

We can all play a part in mitigating climate change and its effects. Here are some steps you can take:

  • Reduce your carbon footprint

    • Use public transportation or bike when possible

    • Switch to energy-efficient appliances

    • Reduce meat consumption and food waste

  • Support renewable energy initiatives

    • Install solar panels on your property

    • Choose green energy providers

    • Invest in renewable energy companies

  • Advocate for climate-friendly policies

    • Contact your local representatives

    • Join environmental advocacy groups

    • Support climate-focused legislation

  • Educate others about rising sea levels

    • Share reliable climate science resources

    • Organize community awareness events

    • Support climate education programs
